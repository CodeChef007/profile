Spiky Sardinian Artichokes

Spiky Sardinian Artichokes have part of the globe artichoke family but very different with very noticeable spikey needles which are very dangerous so be very careful when handling them. The flavor profile of this delicious eatable robust flower is very clean with a hint of earthiness that works well with acidity and fats. Its sweet bitter balance is unmistakable along with its meaty texture, which makes a very enjoyable and versatile ingredient to use when it’s in season. In this picture I’m simple poaching it in a soused liquor which is has a bitter sweet taste infused with various herbs and aromats, once poached I would sauté in a pan with some olive oil, garlic, thyme until a thin golden brown crust is formed.

Beetroot cured salmon

Beetroot cured salmon is one of those classic preparation methods that’s been around for centuries very similar to that of gravlax a Nordic dish. Preparation is straightforward allowing nature to take its course and delivering a very pleasant outcome. I don’t think I can go a summer or even a winter without having a version of cured salmon on the menu an absolute winner.
